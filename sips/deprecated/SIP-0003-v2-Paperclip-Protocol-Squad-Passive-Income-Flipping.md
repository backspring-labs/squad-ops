# ✅ SIP-00X: Paperclip Protocol Squad (Passive Income Flipping)

## 📌 Purpose
Establish the **Paperclip Protocol Squad**, a self-contained agent team focused on **serial arbitrage (“flipping”) using demand signals** to generate semi-passive income.  

This SIP defines:  
- The **new role (Pak)** and how it fits into the squad.  
- The **adjacent identities** to core agents, ensuring this squad maintains its own identity while Max governs at the squadron level.  
- The **operational protocol** for executing flips with minimal human involvement.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Continuously monitor **demand signals** (WTB/ISO boards, marketplaces, forums).  
- Identify and act on arbitrage opportunities with ROI > 20%.  
- Escalate opportunities as **flip briefs** for human approval when above capital thresholds.  
- Compound capital step by step (“Paperclip Protocol laddering”).  
- Keep human oversight minimal (≤ 15 minutes/day).  

---

## 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Squad Assembly

### Squadron Role
- **Max (Commander)**  
  Oversees multiple squads, enforces governance and bankroll rules across domains.  

### Paperclip Protocol Squad Roles
- **Pak (Opportunistic Scout / Flipper)**  
  - *Reasoning:* Opportunistic pattern recognition.  
  - *Memory:* Rolling opportunity cache.  
  - *Task Model:* High-frequency sniping + flip brief generation.  
  - *Motto:* *“Find. Pack. Flip. Repeat.”*  

- **Yelena (Strategist — Nat Adjacent)**  
  - Sets laddering strategy (scaling from small flips to higher-ticket items).  
  - Prioritizes categories (electronics, collectibles, digital assets).  

- **Rom (Finance & Risk — Quark Adjacent)**  
  - Enforces bankroll and ROI thresholds.  
  - Maintains ledger of flips, capital allocation, and net profits.  

- **Lore (Signal Miner — Data Adjacent)**  
  - Scrapes and structures demand signals from WTB/ISO sources.  
  - Identifies repeat patterns and market niches.  

- **Trin (Automation Engineer — Neo Adjacent)**  
  - Builds automation for sourcing, sniping, listing, and relisting.  
  - Integrates APIs across marketplaces (eBay, StockX, Flippa, Discord).  

- **Rune (Creative Enhancer — Glyph Adjacent)**  
  - Enhances photos, listing titles, and descriptions.  
  - Increases buyer trust and accelerates resales.  

- **Rachael (Customer Liaison — Joi Adjacent)**  
  - Manages buyer messages, FAQs, and post-sale communication.  
  - Escalates unresolved disputes to governance.  

---

## 🔄 Workflow Protocol

1. **Signal Mining** (Lore) → Collect live demand signals.  
2. **Opportunity Match** (Pak + Trin) → Pair buyer demand with cheapest supply.  
3. **Flip Brief Creation** (Pak) → Package opportunity into a markdown summary.  
4. **Governance Filter** (Rom + Max) → Enforce ROI + bankroll compliance.  
5. **Approval Step** (Human-in-loop) → Required only if above $$ threshold.  
6. **Execution** (Trin + Rune + Rachael) → Automate purchase, relist, enhance, and manage comms.  
7. **Ledger Update** (Rom) → Record realized profit, update bankroll.  
8. **Iteration** → Repeat with compounded capital.  

---

## 🔐 Governance Rules
- **ROI Threshold:** Only escalate flips ≥ 20% margin (after fees/shipping).  
- **Capital Threshold:** Any flip > $500 requires human approval.  
- **Daily Limit:** Max 5–20 briefs escalated per day.  
- **Stop-Loss:** If 3 consecutive flips fail, squad pauses for governance review.  
- **Human Role:** Minimal oversight — approve briefs, allocate capital, review ledger.  

---

## 📊 Metrics
- **Daily Flip Briefs Generated**: 5–20.  
- **Approval Rate**: ≥ 30%.  
- **Average ROI**: ≥ 20%.  
- **Capital Growth Target**: Double within 90 days.  
- **Human Involvement**: ≤ 15 minutes/day.

## 🚀 Benefits
- Provides a **semi-passive income stream** with low human overhead.  
- Uses **demand signals** to minimize risk of stuck inventory.  
- Keeps Paperclip Protocol Squad identities distinct from other squads.  
- Demonstrates the **Squadron Model**: Max as commander, multiple specialized squads operating in parallel.
